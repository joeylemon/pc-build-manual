Choosing Parts

Choosing the parts to go into your computer is highly dependent on your budget. Obviously, more expensive components will usually yield better performance. After a certain point, however, the performance gains will be marginal. Therefore, we will explore budgets in the range of $500 to $2,000. If you're trying to save money while still having an enjoyable experience, a lower budget such as $500 is definitely doable. On the other hand, if you have money to spare and would really enjoy an extremely high-performance machine, a budget like $1,500 or $2,000 will give you exactly what you want.

At this price range, you will not have a large degree of flexibility when selecting parts, as this is meant to model a budget-focused build. Therefore, this build will focus not only on getting the best performance possible for a low price, but also on being highly upgradable when the time comes.

  • Playing all latest AAA video game titles at 1080p
  • Running reasonably complex physics simulations in Solidworks or similar

  • 3D modeling in Blender or similar applications
  • Training simple neural networks
